All About Mistletoe

1. What is Mistletoe?

Mistletoe is a type of parasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees. It has small, oval-shaped green leaves and white berries. The most famous holiday species are Viscum album (European mistletoe) and Phoradendron leucarpum (American mistletoe).

2. Origins and Mythology

Mistletoe has a rich, ancient history:

  • Celtic & Druid Traditions: A sacred plant believed to bring healing and protection.
  • Norse Mythology: Linked to the story of Balder—giving rise to the kissing tradition.
  • Roman Times: Symbol of peace and reconciliation.

3. Mistletoe and Christmas

Mistletoe became a Christmas symbol in 18th-century England. Hanging mistletoe represents:

  • Love & romance
  • Peace & goodwill
  • Luck & protection

Families hang it in doorways to invite joy and holiday magic.

4. Where Mistletoe Grows

  • Europe: Apple, oak, and poplar trees.
  • North America: Hardwood trees like oak and hickory.
  • Worldwide: Australia, Africa, and Asia have native varieties.
  • Birds spread mistletoe seeds by eating the berries.
  • Mistletoe can live for years on one tree.
  • Some traditions say it protects homes from misfortune.

How to Hang Mistletoe

Where to Hang It

  • Above doorways or arches
  • In the kitchen doorway
  • Above the dining table
  • On a chandelier (away from hot bulbs)
  • Next to the Christmas tree

How to Hang It Securely

  • Use a small clear Command hook
  • Hang with ribbon, twine, or festive string
  • Trim stems neatly
  • Lightly mist fresh mistletoe every few days

Extra Tips

  • Place it where people walk naturally
  • Avoid heaters or vents
  • Berries may drop when ripe
  • Replace ribbon if it twists
